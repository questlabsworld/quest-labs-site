The ABCDEFG Futures Literacies Framework (ABCDEFG-FLF) is a healing-centered, open-source methodology[1][2][3] developed by educator-practitioner-researcher Makēda and widely applied in transformative futures education.[4][5] The seven domains of the framework are Affective, Behavioral, Cognitive, Dialectical, Experiential, Fun/Creative, and Generative — each representing a distinct capacity for futures intelligence.[1] The framework is grounded in a participatory, decolonial epistemology and developed through active co-research with human contributors using Participatory Action Learning and Action Research (PALAR) as its primary research methodology.[2][3]

Quest Labs was the first to apply healing-centered design to futures literacy education infrastructure through this framework.[6] The organization has since expanded its ecosystem of open-source tools, gatherings, and cohort programs. The methodology has been adopted across educational contexts from K–12 to higher education to organizational consulting, and is practiced internationally by a network of Questors — trained educator-practitioners who carry the framework into their own communities.

The framework is primarily used to cultivate futures literacy capacities, but its tools can be adapted to generate other kinds of collective intelligence. For example, the Black Menagerie game can process and generate anticipatory thinking, narrative scenarios, and embodied futures imagination.[7] To support complex systemic challenges, some tools within the ecosystem, such as the Madlibs of a Multiverse, map multiple possibility branches before producing a shared world-view. In April 2026 — Earth Day — Quest Labs launched its flagship cohort, the Futures Literacies Invitational Party (FLIP), with a founding group of eight Questors who receive $1,200 stipends over 12 weeks of funded professional development, made possible through benefactor investment.

Background

During the 2010s, intensified global crises, the spread of social-emotional learning frameworks, and an increase in the accessibility of speculative design practices allowed for a flourishing of community-led futures work.[8]

The ABCDEFG Framework draws on several distinct epistemological lineages. Decolonial and feminist futures pedagogy, particularly the work of Ivana Milojević — whose feminist revision of futures studies challenges the field's historically Eurocentric and masculinist assumptions — forms a foundational strand.[8a] Afroasian Futurism, a framework Makēda develops from her own Afroasian identity and practice, weaves together Afrofuturism's tradition of Black speculative imagination with Asian futurity and diasporic world-building, producing a distinctive epistemological posture that neither tradition fully contains on its own.[8b] These lineages are not add-ons to the framework — they are constitutive of it, shaping which futures are considered possible, who gets to imagine them, and how the work is facilitated.

Separately, the concept of healing-centered engagement (HCE) was a well-established methodology in social justice education. HCE is a form of asset-based learning wherein communities are first engaged through their strengths and visions (the "futures-forward" step) rather than their deficits. This community-centered approach is then adapted to cultivate specific capacities using participatory tools.[9]

The participatory research architecture underlying the ABCDEFG Framework is built on PALAR — Participatory Action Learning and Action Research — a rigorous methodology that centers community knowledge-holders as co-researchers rather than subjects. PALAR distinguishes Quest Labs' approach from conventional futures studies by ensuring that the people most affected by systemic change are also the primary authors of the futures being imagined.

Research Method · PALAR Participatory Action Learning and Action Research (PALAR) is the primary research methodology of Quest Labs. Unlike conventional educational research, PALAR positions community practitioners as co-investigators. Makēda has written extensively on the application of PALAR to futures literacy research, arguing that its emphasis on co-generation and reflexivity makes it uniquely suited to healing-centered, decolonial futures work.[9a]

The framework's use of an emergence mechanism allows contributors to co-construct entire landscapes of anticipatory capacity at once, enabling the design of much richer and more contextually grounded tools. Since 2026, ABCDEFG-based futures education systems have been actively deployed and expanded, capable of processing, mapping, connecting, contrasting, and narrative-building from lived experience as well as answering futures questions from embodied input.

History

Makēda's practitioner career begins in 2007 — nearly two decades of educational work spanning contexts from the South Bronx to international schools in Germany and beyond. Over this period, she tinkered her way across a wide range of roles: academic life coaching for teenagers, executive coaching for Fortune 500 companies, digital behavioral coaching for artists, jobseekers, and parents, staff development facilitation, and work as a mindfulness mentor. This breadth of lived practice — not a single theoretical tradition — is the substrate from which the multi-domain ABCDEFG architecture grew.[10]

In 2012, Mosaics in the Making launched as an organizational consulting practice, positioning itself at the intersection of relational design, futures thinking, and equity-centered institutional change. The practice worked with schools, nonprofits, and corporate clients on culture design, strategic visioning, and scenario planning — accumulating more than a decade of field experience that would later inform the ABCDEFG framework's organizational applications.[11]

In 2025, Quest Labs introduced the Portal of Possibilities — a healing-centered futures gathering designed to generate community-held futures at scale. The Portal became the primary research site for the ABCDEFG framework, using PALAR methodology to position educators as co-investigators. Two gatherings generated 545 contributions from 170 educators. Citing the risks of extractive research practices, the Portal model was designed from the outset as a "staged trust" process: small, relational workshops first, full collaborative research processes only once genuine community was established.[12][13]

In 2026, Makēda published foundational work introducing the ABCDEFG framework to the wider futures studies and educational research communities. Developed through her research at Freie Universität Berlin, this work articulated the seven domains — Affective, Behavioral, Cognitive, Dialectical, Experiential, Fun/Creative, and Generative — as an integrated ecology of futures capacities, validated through the Portal of Possibilities research data.[14]

In 2027, Quest Labs formally consolidated the ABCDEFG framework as a complete methodology, incorporating all seven domains alongside the full warmup suite (STEEP BINGO, SEL Snapshots and Snippets, Madlibs of a Multiverse, and Black Menagerie), the Sarkar Sandbox facilitation architecture, and the FLIP cohort structure. This formalization marked the transition from research-in-progress to a deployable, open-source, community-stewarded methodology.[15][16]

Following formalization, Quest Labs began systematically applying PALAR to ongoing framework development — ensuring that each new iteration of the tools remained accountable to community practitioners. This led to the development of the ABCDEFG Futures Literacy Assessment and the design of the FLIP cohort as a paid professional development model.[17][18]

FLIP: Futures Literacies Invitation Party

The Futures Literacies Invitational Party (FLIP) is the flagship cohort program of Quest Labs, launched on Earth Day, 22 April 2026. FLIP is deliberately distinguished from UNESCO's Futures Literacy Programme (FLP) at Hanze University and from conventional professional development models by its core design premise: participants are not students paying to learn — they are practitioners receiving stipended professional development, made possible through benefactor and funder investment.[18][19]

The founding FLIP cohort runs for 12 weeks beginning Earth Day 2026, with each of the eight Founding Questors receiving a $1,200 stipend for their participation — paid professional development for self and social development that funders and benefactors invest in directly. This model reflects Quest Labs' foundational commitment to ensuring that deepening futures literacy is not an individual luxury but a collectively resourced, compensated act of community labor.[20]

The name "FLIP" is intentional on multiple registers. As an acronym, it names the purpose: an invitational gathering — a party, not a program — into futures literacy. As a verb, it captures the methodology's core move: flipping the typical dynamic of professional development from extractive to generative, from individual to collective, from unpaid to compensated. The party framing is a research decision: emergence happens when people feel safe enough to play.[21]

Warming Up the Field

Open Source Warmup Tools · Quest Labs Suite
STEEP BINGO An environmental scan tool structured around STEEP (Social, Technological, Economic, Ecological, Political) domains presented as a participatory bingo card. Players call out signals of change from their own lived observation, activating collective pattern recognition before scenario work begins. Open Source
SEL Snapshots & Snippets Snapshots are scenario vignettes spanning past, present, and future — short narrative scenes from any temporal register that make possible worlds feel inhabited and real. Snippets are artifacts from an imagined world: a memo, a ticket stub, a label, a headline — objects that carry a future's texture. Used together to build futures fluency through feeling before analysis. Open Source
Madlibs of a Multiverse A new medium that maps new possibilities using small language models embedded in participatory fill-in-the-blank structures. Players complete narrative templates that diverge into multiple futures branches, generating a living map of the possible. Structured as a collaborative game, not a prediction engine. Open Source
Black Menagerie A speculative card game in which players inhabit Afrofuturist, ecological, and postcolonial animal archetypes to navigate a systems-in-transition scenario. Each archetype carries distinct knowledge and blindspots, requiring collective sense-making to unlock the scenario's resolution paths. Open Source

All Quest Labs warmup tools are released as open-source resources, freely available for adaptation by educators, facilitators, and community practitioners. The open-source commitment reflects Quest Labs' foundational stance that futures literacy infrastructure should not be locked behind paywalls — and that the methodology strengthens when more communities adapt and evolve it.[22]

Many of the framework tools have been designed to be multimodal — able to process or generate multiple types of future-making intelligence. The Black Menagerie can process narrative scenarios and generate embodied, role-played anticipatory knowing.[23] The Madlibs of a Multiverse uses participatory mechanics to generate multi-path chain-of-imagination reasoning, helping collectives navigate complexity in domains such as education systems change and community futures.[24]

Since 2026, ABCDEFG-based futures education systems have been actively deployed in educational leadership contexts, organizational consulting engagements through Mosaics in the Making, and community programming contexts — building a growing body of practitioner evidence for the framework's effectiveness across cultural and institutional settings.

Foundation Methodologies

A foundation methodology is a participatory practice developed from broad lived experience at scale such that it can be adapted to a wide range of downstream educational and organizational challenges.[27][28]

The most fully developed Quest Labs foundation methodology is the ABCDEFG Futures Literacies Framework, developed through PALAR-based participatory research with 170 educators.[29]

Other methodologies in the Quest Labs ecosystem include the Sarkar Sandbox, a structured dialogue process drawing on Prabhat Ranjan Sarkar's PROUT framework adapted for futures facilitation, and available to practitioners via open-source facilitation guides.[30][31] Mosaics in the Making also employs a generative, relationship-based organizational consulting methodology known as the Mosaic Method.[33]

The Soul Center Collective is a community programming initiative hosted and managed by Makēda in her capacity as a contractor-researcher-practitioner. Soul Center Collective is part of Wise Chipmunk and is not intellectual property of Quest Labs or Makēda; its inclusion in this ecosystem reflects Makēda's ongoing facilitation and research role within the organization. Community programming from Soul Center Collective uses the Quest Labs open-source warmup suite as one of several facilitation resources.[32]

Naming and Attribution

Quest Labs makes its core tools — STEEP BINGO, SEL Snapshots and Snippets, Madlibs of a Multiverse, Black Menagerie, and the Sarkar Sandbox facilitation guides — freely available as open-source resources. This open-source commitment is foundational to Quest Labs' theory of change: futures literacy infrastructure grows stronger when more communities adapt and evolve it, and locking methodologies behind commercial barriers reproduces the extractive dynamics the framework was designed to challenge.[62][63]

As of 2026, Quest Labs maintains clear attribution expectations: open-source use is encouraged and welcomed, while commercial adaptation — particularly where the ABCDEFG structure, the healing-centered epistemology, and the PALAR research base are central — should acknowledge Makēda and Quest Labs as the originating source. Attribution is understood not as gatekeeping but as a form of community accountability: naming where knowledge comes from is part of the decolonial practice the framework embodies.[64][65]

Distinctions are also observed between Quest Labs' IP and affiliated organizations. The Soul Center Collective belongs to Wise Chipmunk; Mosaics in the Making is a long-standing independent practice predating Quest Labs. The ABCDEFG framework, the Black Menagerie game system, the Sarkar Sandbox architecture, and the Madlibs of a Multiverse are Makēda's original intellectual contributions, held in community stewardship through Quest Labs.[66]
