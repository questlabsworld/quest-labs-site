The situation

The script we're all
still reading from

The world we're being asked to prepare students for keeps arriving faster than our nervous systems, and the systems built to support us, were ever designed to handle. Most offerings meet educators right there, in that weight, and add more to carry.

There IS something different. This is what we reach for.

Who belongs here

The whole adult
ecosystem

Classroom teachers. Instructional coaches. Curriculum designers. Principals. Assistant principals. Department heads. District leads. Learning experience designers. If you touch the conditions in which young people learn, this room is for you.

You are not broken. Neither is the system. It is working exactly as it was designed. You, however, are paying attention. That's why you're here.

Each cohort is capped at 20 to keep it real. Intimate. Honest. Diverse.

The Winded Visionary
You believe in futures thinking. You believe in your students. You're running on fumes from trying to do transformative work inside a system that wasn't built for it. This is a place to put down the performance and pick up a practice.
The Curious Skeptic
You've seen "transformative PD" before. Your caution is earned. Come see what happens when the room is actually built differently. If it doesn't land for you, your feedback is the gift.
The Already-Doing-It
You're already running futures-oriented practice in your classroom or school. You need community, language, and a place to go deeper. There are more of us than you think.
The Systems Thinker
Curriculum director. Instructional coach. School leader navigating more than anyone should have to right now. You sit at the intersection of what is and what could be. This practice is built for people who think in ecosystems.
The practice

What actually
happens

Flip the Script runs on a 12-week arc, adapted specifically for educators and educational leaders. Each session is a living laboratory. A place where the moment you are trying to meet becomes the curriculum. Capped at 20 people so the room stays real.

01

Arrival ritual

One word check-in. You land before you learn.

02

World activation

Enter the week's futures frame. Different physics every time.

03

Main quest

Sarkar Sandbox, futures methods, and productivity practices woven into community crafting of the conditions to embody new cultures and infrastructures of care.

04

Me / We / Re-search

Something solo. Something shared. Something that carries. The sequence is the practice.

05

Artifact of the week

You leave with something made. A key. A map. A seed.

Go fast, go alone. Go further, go together. We're all in this together.

The framework

Seven literacies.
One ecology.

Most futures literacy frameworks center cognitive sense-making. The ABCDEFG Framework works across a fuller range. Because educators don't just think about the future. They feel it, embody it, play with it, and generate it alongside their students.

AAffective
BBehavioral
CCognitive
DDialectical
EExperiential
FFun / Creative
GGenerative

Emerged from 545 contributions by 170 educators across two healing-centered futures gatherings. Not a curriculum. A living methodology. Explore the foundations ↗

How we hold it

The norms that make
the room possible

The story stays. The learning leaves.

What gets shared in the room is held in the room. What shifts in you travels with you everywhere.

Me-search before we-search before re-search

You are the first site of inquiry. Solo reflection before dyad exchange before collective sense-making. Every session, every time.

Play is rigorous

This is not a break from serious work. This is serious work in a different register. The experience is the gift. The play is the point.

We don't perpetuate legacies of labor here

You are not here to produce. You are not here to perform. You are here to practice. The pace is the practice.
